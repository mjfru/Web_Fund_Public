Introduction to HTML - Notes & Small Tasks

Here's the obligatory 'Hello World!'

This page introduces us to various types of tags, which describe the content they contain. They include:

  • Headings and paragraphs of text
  • Images
  • Links
  • Lists (like this one!)
  • Tables
  • Forms

For HTML to be valid, it must contain at least the 'html', 'head', and body tags. **Note to self, look up how to include < & > without creating a tag...

Nesting is the act of encapsulating other tags and are intended with a tab to make the document easier to read. The hierarchy that rises from nesting is called DOM (Document Object Model)

To comment in HTML, the format is < ! -- Comment Here -- > (without spaces, of course!)

Text Elements

Some of the most common elements include:

  • Headers
  • The h1-h6 tags, with h1 being the most important and only to be used once
  • Paragraphs of text, < p >
  • Hyperlink or anchor tags, < a >, must also include href, which provides the ur in quotes
  • Emphasis < em > & strong < strong > to be used sparingly
